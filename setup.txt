Consider a soccer match. If we have a matrix for the number of goals scored by team A and team B, where the index (i,j) gives the probability that team A scores i goals and team B scores j goals (0-indexed matrix):

- we know that the summation over the probabilities of all elements are 1 (valid PMF). It's safe to limit the size of the matrix to something like 10 by 10 as larger indices have roughly 0 probability.
- We can model the score probability with PMF P(X_a = i, X_b = j).
- We can also get priors on P(X_a) and P(X_b) based on their previous matches (not the matches they played against each other).
- For simplicity we can assume the random variable X_a and X_b are independent.

For a new match between team A and B, we want to predict the probability that (team A wins), i.e. sum over all elements with i > j, 0 (tie) i=j, and 2(team B wins) i.e. j > i, therefore these predictions should sum to 1.

Let's assume we don't have any data that we can use to estimate the joint PMF and all we have are priors on individual PMFs.
